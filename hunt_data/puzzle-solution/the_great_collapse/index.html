Solution to The Great Collapse

Author: Eben Olson

Overview

This puzzle involves folding and combining (addition mod 26) grids of letters to obtain a list of 36 words.

These words can be combined into triplets according to the pattern (WORD, HOMOPHONE, SYNONYM) i.e. (FLOWER, FLOUR, BLOOM).

The 18 words generated by this matching can again be combined in triplets to yield another list of 6 words.

These can be sorted into 3 pairs by the same pattern, and the resulting words can be combined to yield the answer word, OVOID.

Following the pattern (Homophone, Synonym) → WORD, the following sets of words can be extracted from those found above:

Left & Top

(DYNE, FEAST)→DINE
(HAUL, FOYER)→HALL
(HIM, CANTICLE)→HYMN
(LACKS, LOOSE)→LAX
(SOME, TOTAL)→SUM
(WHEN, CYST)→WEN

Left & Right

(FOWLS, POLLUTES)→FOULS
(HEART, STAG)→HART
(PEACE, PORTION)→PIECE
(RUED, UNCIVIL)→RUDE
(SURF, PEASANT)→SERF
(RING, TWIST)→WRING

Left & Bottom

(BUOY, CHILD)→BOY
(DUEL, PAIRED)→DUAL
(LONE, ADVANCE)→LOAN
(LIAR, HARP)→LYRE
(WRAP, WHACK)→RAP
(TOW, DIGIT)→TOE

Each of these sets of 6 contains two triplets of equal length. These triplets can be added mod 26 as follows:

LAX+SUM+WEN=BAY
DUAL+LOAN+LYRE=BITE
BOY+RAP+TOE=NET
HART+RUDE+SERF=SANE
FOULS+PIECE+WRING=SPICE
DINE+HALL+HYMN=TIME

This set of 6 words can again be paired by the same rule

(BITE, BAY)→BIGHT
(SANE, NET)→SEINE
(TIME, SPICE)→THYME

Finally, these 3 words can be combined to obtain the answer:

BIGHT+SEINE+THYME=OVOID
